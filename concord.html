    • Overview

    For my first Bloc assignment in their program we were tasked with creating a file sharing, bookmarking, and collaboration website. Blocbox which is known as Concord was a true test of my abilities as a designer. Alot of our deliverables were given to help us maintain pace and reach a certain goal.

    The Challenge

    Creating a website that was unique. I wanted something that was different from my classmate’s submissions. This was my first take on using what I learned and blending it with my own personality.

    The Answer

    I wanted to convey the message of fun and playful while maintaining a simplistic and modern design. An app where businesses and users can go to collaborate, bookmark, and share content they find online as well as other personal things. All of this was the force leading me to get huge amounts of inspiration from our competitors and use that to build a great product.

    Competitive Analysis

    Discord was my main inspiration for shaping Concord into what it is today. Discord has a solid marketing pitch on their website with a brilliant layout, a catchy name, and a good color scheme. I ran with those ideas in mind and came up with Concord.


    Evernote was the side competition. Its what drove the file saving and organizing aspect of my entire website. So I wanted to take what they’ve succeeded most with and try to spin it in my own creative way. Concluding with a more friendly and fun take for users of all ages.

    Personas

I stuck with some ideas that felt right to me but changed my home page of Concord the most.

    Mockups / First Edition

    The user research conducted was arguing for more bolder hue’s of purple. Also the settings section of Concord’s main dashboard would be hard to see if the page fills up with boxes. A lot of this would be fixed later on, especially with the future hope that this would be a more simple fix by adding a pop up tutorial.



    Roadblock & Solution

    After conducting a preference test and getting a majority vote of the newly designed home page below, all of the pieces came together. The dashboard got a facelift and so did the smaller pages. Once again relying on user testing is the key to any successful design project and Concord is the perfect example of that.

    Branding

    The branding started with finding a logo started with symmetrical letters but then was followed by shape design. After I saw what a wireframe image looks like it hit me. That’s when I landed on the Concord box logo. It shows creation can happen by finding inspiration in the smaller things.


    Belgrano and Open Sans were the first fonts I chose. After the preference test of the two home pages, I used more of Concord's logo font throughout the page instead. It really gave it a more modern and up to date look without looking like Evernote or one of the competitors.


    The purple and gold colors came from big inspiration after seeing the Sketch application website. It’s the first palette that popped into my head giving me the idea to really use purple throughout the whole project.

    The User Testing

    My school, Bloc, handed us a majority of the deliverables for our Blocbox (Concord) project. Therefore what helped the most was preference testing everything I could. As you can see below, I created two different home pages, two different dashboards, and settings tweaks that would later turn into the final project that you see.


    The Results on my home page preference test were just what I expected. After looking at the original design for long enough, I decided to make some font and color tweaks. My first home page design was using a lot of the deliverables and design techniques my school taught me. That changed when I went to revisit the design after a couple months. Bolder hues, better font use, and an overall more compelling design led the users to decide on this design rather than my original.


    The Results for my dashboard preference test went differently. I created a top navigation dashboard originally and wanted to add something different in the mix. Creating a right navigation dashboard only led to confusion and wasted time. Users were saying how hard it would be for responsive coding, but loved the cleaner look of it. Leading to a struggle, it was only natural to take the majority vote here and stick with the original design leading to bolder purples and other minor tweaks to make the whole project come together nicely.

    Conclusion

    What didn’t work

    Coding websites through HTML and CSS is not one of my strengths. I really had to work for this one. Pages were buggy and full of messy layout code. I had this grand scheme of how it needed to look and not delivering on the coding side of taught me a big lesson.

    What I would've done differently

    Trying to not go through as many iterations of the home page and dashboard that I did. Everything else was spot on but those were the main struggles, both on the coding side and designing side.

    How I will use this information in the future

    Preference tests and user testing helped save me from some bad design choices and looks. I want to use that to the best of my abilities in the future and on future projects.
