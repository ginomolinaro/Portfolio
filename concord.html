Overview

For my first Bloc assignment in their program we were tasked with creating a file sharing, bookmarking, and collaboration website. Blocbox which is known as Concord was a true test of my abilities as a designer. Alot of our deliverables were given to help us maintain pace and reach a certain goal.

The Challenge

Creating a website that was unique. I wanted something that was different from my classmate’s submissions. This was my first take on using what I learned and blending it with my own personality. 

       
The Answer

       I wanted to convey the message of fun and playful while maintaining a
       simplistic and modern design. An app where businesses and users can go
       to collaborate, bookmark, and share content they find online as well
       as other personal things. All of this was the force leading me to get huge
       amounts of inspiration from our competitors and use that to build a great
       product.

A bookmark centered design will be the main goal here because the responses show a lot of people use bookmark tools to save content. It needs to be convenient and easy to use like Microsoft Note or Google Keep. As well making it useful on iPhones and androids.

Designers, Web developers, and other technology related fields seem to use these tools more. More work is done in a collaborative environment and sharing resources with co-workers is a must. Concord needed to be an app you could use anywhere.

Competitive Analysis

Discord was my main inspiration for shaping Concord into what it is today. Discord has a solid marketing pitch on their website with a brilliant layout, a catchy name, and a good color scheme. I ran with those ideas in mind and came up with Concord.

Evernote was the side competition. Its what drove the file saving and organizing aspect of my entire website. So I wanted to take what they’ve succeeded most with and try to spin it in my own creative way. Concluding with a more friendly and fun take for users of all ages.

Personas

I felt it was essential to stick to what I know and not deviate to try and think of users that don’t fit the userbase. Their goals and frustrations really helped me get inspired for the design and layouts to come. It also helped me with some of the core features and buttons that were needed.

User Stories & Flow

This was going to be for my login, settings, and miscellanous features so that I can try to flex more of my artistic capabilities. Trying to explain to the user that its more than just a simple note sharing website and that it has everything you need to feel comfortable to keep you coming back.

Wireframes

Consistency is what I wanted throughout this whole project. In some ways it worked and some ways it didn’t. The wireframes came out very differently than my finished product, and based on user testing, that changed it again. I stuck with some ideas that felt right to me but changed my home page of Concord the most.

Mockups / First edition

The user research conducted was arguing for more bolder hue’s of purple. Also the settings section of Concord’s main dashboard would be hard to see if the page fills up with boxes. A lot of this would be fixed later on, especially with the future hope that this would be a more simple fix by adding a pop up tutorial.

Mockups / Roadblock & Solution

After conducting a preference test and getting a majority vote of the newly designed home page below, all of the pieces came together. The dashboard got a facelift and so did the smaller pages. Once again relying on user testing is the key to any successful design project and Concord is the perfect example of that.

Branding

The branding started with finding a logo started with symmetrical letters but then was followed by shape design. After I saw what a wireframe image looks like it hit me. That’s when I landed on the Concord box logo. It shows creation can happen by finding inspiration in the smaller things.

Belgrano and Open Sans were the first fonts I chose. After the preference test of the two home pages, I used more of Concord's logo font throughout the page instead. It really gave it a more modern and up to date look without looking like Evernote or one of the competitors.

The purple and gold colors came from big inspiration after seeing the Sketch application website. It’s the first palette that popped into my head giving me the idea to really use purple throughout the whole project.

Conclusion

    What didn’t work  

    Coding websites through HTML and CSS is not one of my strengths. I really
    had to work for this one. Pages were buggy and full of messy layout code.
    I had this grand scheme of how it needed to look and not delivering on
    the coding side of taught me a big lesson.

    What would I have done differently  

    Trying to not go through as many iterations of the home page and dashboard
    that I did. Everything else was spot on but those were the main struggles,
    both on the coding side and designing side.

    How will I use this information in the future  

    Preference tests and user testing helped save me from some bad design
    choices and looks. I want to use that to the best of my abilities in
    the future and on future projects.
