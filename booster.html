    • Overview

    Plagued by the abundance of crowdfunding websites, I wanted to create a virtual stage to give underground internet content creators and their fans a place to go.

    The Challenge

    Originally, Booster was going to be a crowdfunding website for the entertainment industry but the competition was polluted. The trouble was how could I spin this and make it different.

    The Answer

    In a game show fashion the user will decide the creator’s fate. Updated monthly, Booster will review the newest pool of talent from platforms such as Youtube, Snapchat, Twitch, and film. Booster will then place them on the front page for user’s to vote through in an upvote and downvote system. If the creators get enough votes by the end of the month they will get rewarded. The prizes will range from new investors, cash, or even up to 1000 new fans.

    Competitive Analysis

    Kickstarter is for entertainers and generally supports new and unique ideas. Gumroad is not much of a competitor but would be selling things to a certain viewer kind of like Booster. Social sharing gives Gumroad a unique advantage to stand out.


    Patreon is the main competitor for Booster. They are heavily entertainment driven for all creative types and hit on the same user base that we do. They have a system that works and a solid website.


    GoFundMe is a competitor known by their name, not by website design or demographic. They are the most generic crowdfunding site out there. IndieGoGo is great for the underground scene. They could prove to be a big competitor because they appeal to the startup industry, and also very investor friendly.

    Branding

    Heavy inspiration came from my mood board that I created. Neon-underground was the original idea but after further testing, it was better off going with a more natural, minimilistic, and clean feel.


    The colors changed from neon pinks and purples to lighter blue and red, giving it much more personality. Adding shapes is what gave it character. Learning how to fight with this process and the struggling contrasts, I landed on more greys and whites based on more user research.


    I kept the logo design because the underground feeling made sense to me and is where I come from as a person. I like to see things emerge and become successful.

    The User Testing

    My user testing reults for Booster proved how, for big projects, you need as many eyes as you can get. My mentor for Bloc made sure that I get at least a few Skype sessions in, or face to face meetings if I could, so that I could really get the most out of the user testing. It proved useful to say the least, giving me the exact results I needed to shape Booster into my favorite project I've ever worked on.


    The Results had a lot to do with learning how to deal with making big changes and using them to my advantage. Gathering the results together and seeing what I could change. The biggest change was the project details page. Going from a completely different color switch was a tricky process but the most rewarding. Also navigation and color issues continue to be the biggest takeaway for me and what I need to work on most as a new designer.

    Conclusion

    What I've learned

    At one point I thought it was all going to fall apart. Making sure I came out on top after hitting a roadblock of inspiration was a major key. Having my original product not work out taught me a lot about slowing down. It took a lot to get it right and big projects like these can't be rushed.

    What I would've done differently

    Given the proper amount of time I would have loved to expand on the user settings, an interactive video for the front pages, as well as adding a pop up tutorial that would help users figure out the mechanics.

    How I will use this information in the future

    I learned to trust what others had to say, while also steering clear of feedback that may not have helped. I know where my skills are as a designer, I’ve pinpointed a lot of my strengths and weaknesses while also exploring some new territories while building out Booster.
